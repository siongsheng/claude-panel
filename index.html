What it is

A thin loop-engineering layer, not another reviewer.

panel doesn't reinvent planning, TDD, worktrees, or debugging — it composes the plugins that do, and adds the one thing they lack: the loop that wires them together with gates.

Its value isn't a model or a prompt — it's control flow: what blocks, what only advises, and where the loop stops and hands back to a human.

Scope

What it does — and deliberately doesn't.

Does

  • +Gate every change on deterministic two-commit TDD.
  • +Review each PR with two model families.
  • +Triage before fixing; separate inherited from new debt.
  • +Keep one findings ledger per PR, always posted.
  • +Wire a repo's CI with /panel-init.

Doesn't

  • −Auto-merge — a human always does.
  • −Block on AI reviewers — only the TDD gate and CI block.
  • −Reinvent planning, TDD, or worktrees — it composes them.
  • −Index your whole repo — it reviews the diff.
  • −Replace your judgment — it's a discipline, not an autopilot.

Audience

Who it's for.

A good fit

  • →Claude Code devs who want a disciplined, prevention-first PR flow.
  • →Teams wanting cross-model review + a deterministic TDD gate in CI.
  • →Anyone who wants the agent loop engineered, not YOLO.

Not the tool if you want

  • ✕Autonomous auto-merging, no human in the loop.
  • ✕Zero-ceremony hacking where any gate is friction.
  • ✕A codebase where you can't or won't run tests.
